What's the difference between VPS and shared hosting?

Shared hosting puts dozens of sites on one server with shared CPU, RAM, and disk. A VPS gives you dedicated, guaranteed resources — your own slice of hardware that nobody else can eat into. You get root access, isolation, and consistent performance regardless of what other customers are doing.

Do I get full root SSH access?

Yes, on every plan. From the moment your VPS is provisioned you receive SSH credentials with full root privileges. You can install packages, configure firewalls, run cron jobs, and modify the OS however you like.

Which operating systems are available?

We offer Ubuntu 20.04 / 22.04, Debian 11 / 12, CentOS Stream 9, and AlmaLinux 9 as standard images. You can also request a custom ISO if you need a specific distro not on the list.

Can I upgrade my VPS plan later?

Absolutely. You can upgrade RAM, vCPU, and storage at any time from your client portal. Upgrades apply instantly with a brief restart. Downgrading is also available at your next billing cycle.

Are snapshots and backups included?

VPS Pro includes free weekly snapshots. VPS Business adds daily snapshots, and VPS Elite includes fully managed backups with 30-day retention. You can also create manual snapshots on demand from the control panel at any time.
